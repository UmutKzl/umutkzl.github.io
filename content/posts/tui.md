I'm a terminal-lover guy. I love terminal too much.
Today, I'll introduce you some of my favourite TUI apps in this text.

## browsh

A Firefox-based web browser for your terminal. I know, it's crazy.

## btop

Task manager for your terminal. It has a beautiful interface and it's very useful.

## fzf

Fuzzy-finder for your terminal.
It helps you to find files, directories, and even commands in your terminal.

## lazygit

I know, git sometimes can be confusing. Should I push, which files?
It's really good but complicated at the same time.
That's why we have lazygit. You can add, commit, push and stash your files.

## neovim

IDE for your terminal.
It's really bad at vanilla but if you configure it, it's really good.
You can do everything in it. It's just a IDE for your terminal.

## helix

If you can't configure neovim, then use helix.
helix is a modern text editor for your terminal.
It's really good and it's really easy to use.

## tmux

Terminal multiplexer.
It allows you to split your terminal into multiple panes and windows.
You can even copy a text without leaving your keyboard. Crazy, right?

## lqp

Query JSON files without leaving your terminal.

## posting

Posting is Postman for your terminal. It can do simple things Postman do.

## bat

Cat (command) but with syntax highlighting and git integration.
It's really good for reading code in your terminal.

## yazi

File manager for your terminal.
It has a beautiful interface and it's really easy to use.

## lazydocker

You can manage your docker containers, images, volumes and networks from your terminal!
No need for messy commands.
